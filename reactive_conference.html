Day 2

Life Beyond the Illusion of Present

The first session in Day 2 is from Jonas Boner, who is the CTO and co-founder of typesafe and was creator of akka actor. He talks about the time as first class and mentions that relying on timestamp ia bad idea. CRUD is dead. No UD anymore only create and read.

Event Processing: Making sense of the fire-hose in real-time

The second session is from Andy Piper .

In the Presentation, Andy Piper talks about Event Stream Processing (ESP). He tries to answer the following questions: what is ESP? Why it is needed? What does it look like? And the design considerations like performance (needs to be fast), resiliency, scalablity and distribution. In the end, he mentions fast data architecture and several technologies like Apache Storm, Spark, Kafaka, etc.

Reactive, Event Driven User Interfaces

The third one is quite interesting.

Error handling in Reactive Systems.

This fourth session is from Dean Wampler .

In this presentation, Dean Wampler thinks Failure should be first class. He mentions a number of ways to do failure handling, like the most common failure handling mixed with domain logic to actor's "let it crash" way. He also compares CSP (communicating sequential process, examples like Go & Clojure's core.async) and Actor and concludes that they can implement each other although they appear different. In the end, he tries to response some critisms on actor.
